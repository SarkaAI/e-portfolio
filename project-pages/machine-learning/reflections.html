Reflections
Personal reflections on what I learned and how my thinking evolved during the Machine Learning module.
Overview
This module significantly reshaped my understanding of machine learning, moving it from a purely technical discipline to a broader socio-technical system that requires critical thinking, contextual awareness, and responsible application.
At the start of the module, my perception of machine learning was largely focused on models and outputs. I understood algorithms conceptually, but I had not fully appreciated the importance of data quality, the limitations of models, or the risks associated with their real-world use. As the module progressed, particularly through hands-on work and collaborative discussions, my perspective evolved into a more critical and applied understanding.
Working with Data
One of the most important areas of learning for me was the role of datasets. Working with the Airbnb dataset highlighted how real-world data is rarely clean or complete. Decisions such as removing incorrect entries, handling missing values, and transforming variables were not simply technical steps but required judgement. For example, replacing missing review data with zero values reflected an assumption about listing activity, which could influence model behaviour. This made me realise that preprocessing decisions are inherently interpretive and can introduce bias if not carefully considered.
I also learned that dataset structure strongly determines model effectiveness. In our analysis, location and room type were dominant predictors of price, while many numerical features showed weak correlations. This challenged my initial assumption that more variables automatically improve models. Instead, I learned that feature relevance and representation are far more important than quantity. Additionally, the imbalance across boroughs introduced limitations in model reliability, particularly for underrepresented areas. This reinforced the importance of understanding dataset distribution before drawing conclusions.
Applying Models
Applying machine learning techniques further deepened my understanding. The linear regression model achieved moderate predictive performance, explaining around half of the variance in price. While this initially seemed acceptable, further analysis revealed important limitations. Residual patterns showed that the model struggled with high-value listings and local variations, indicating that real-world problems are often more complex than linear assumptions allow. This highlighted the importance of not over-relying on performance metrics alone, but instead interpreting results critically.
Clustering provided another perspective on the data. Identifying four market segments offered useful business insights, particularly in understanding different types of listings and pricing strategies. However, the relatively low silhouette score demonstrated that these segments were not clearly separated. This was a key learning moment, as it showed that even when models produce interpretable outputs, those outputs may oversimplify reality. I learned that model usefulness depends not only on results but also on how accurately those results represent underlying structures.
Broader Implications
The discussions on AI writers and machine learning risks further expanded my understanding beyond technical implementation. I became more aware of issues such as hallucinations, bias, and over-reliance on automated systems. A key insight was that machine learning models generate outputs based on statistical patterns rather than true understanding. This creates a gap between perceived intelligence and actual reliability. In professional contexts, particularly high-risk domains, this gap can have serious consequences. As a result, I now view machine learning systems as tools that require continuous human oversight rather than autonomous solutions.
Collaboration and Contribution
From a collaborative perspective, the team project was an important part of my learning. Working in a virtual environment required coordination, clear communication, and an understanding of team roles. One challenge was aligning different approaches and ensuring consistency across analysis and reporting. However, this process also strengthened my ability to integrate multiple perspectives and contribute to a shared objective. I learned that technical work in machine learning is rarely done in isolation and that collaboration is essential for producing meaningful outcomes.
Reflecting on my contributions, I believe I added value by focusing on analytical clarity and critical interpretation of results. Rather than only describing outputs, I aimed to explain what they meant in context and highlight limitations. This aligns with the broader goal of moving from technical execution to applied understanding. At the same time, I recognise that there is still room for improvement, particularly in exploring more advanced modelling techniques and deepening my statistical intuition.
Professional Development and Action Plan
The module also contributed to my professional development. It strengthened several key skills, including critical thinking, problem-solving, and data interpretation. It also improved my ability to communicate technical concepts clearly, both in written discussions and collaborative work. Importantly, it reinforced the need to consider ethical and practical implications when working with machine learning systems.
Looking forward, this experience will influence how I approach machine learning in the future. I will place greater emphasis on understanding data before modelling, carefully evaluating model assumptions, and considering the broader context in which systems are deployed. I am also more aware of the importance of explainability and transparency, particularly in environments where decisions have real-world impact.
Conclusion
This module provided a well-rounded learning experience that combined technical application with critical reflection. It challenged my initial assumptions, deepened my understanding of machine learning, and developed skills that are directly relevant to professional practice. Most importantly, it shifted my perspective from viewing machine learning as a set of tools to understanding it as a complex system that requires thoughtful and responsible use.
