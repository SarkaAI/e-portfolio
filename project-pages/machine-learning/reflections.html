Reflections
Personal reflections on what I learned and how my thinking evolved during the Machine Learning module.
Overview
This module broadly changed my perception on machine learning from mere technical domain to being a socio-technical system that demands higher understanding in terms of problems and solutions and responsible use of systems.
At the beginning of the module, my understanding of machine learning revolved around models and results. I had an understanding of algorithms, however, I had not understood the underlying concept behind quality of the data, the inherent limitations of the models or the risks associated with their real-world use. As the module continued, particularly via hands-on learning and classroom discussions and seminars, my perspective evolved into a more critical and applied understanding.
Working with Data
The role of datasets was such a big learning area for me. We learned from the Airbnb dataset that real-life data is rarely clean and complete. Choices like eliminating invalid entries, dealing with missing values and updating different variables, were about more than just getting data to look right, but they required judgement on our part. By substituting or removing zero values in the missing review data, you would make assumptions about the listing activity( no review could mean a newer listing or an inactive listing) which could affect the model behaviour. This made me to understand the preprocessing decision is strictly interpreted, and potentially biased.
I also discovered how dataset form shapes the model performance of great effect. By our analysis, location and room type were dominant predictors of price, while many numerical features showed marginal correlations. At first, I didn't really believe that more variables would automatically improve your model with different variables. Instead, what I learned was that the relevance and the representation of features are far more important than quantity by a wide margin. Similarly, the imbalance he imbalance across boroughs introduced limitations in model reliability, especially in underrepresented communities. This emphasized the need to know the distribution of the dataset before acting upon it.
Applying Models
To have to work hands-on with machine learning techniques deepened my understanding even more. The linear regression model had a modest predictive ability which explained some 50% of the variance in price. Although this seemed acceptable initially, it became very limited. In the sample of residual patterns, it was shown that, particularly high-value listings and local variation, the model struggled even more, suggesting the fact that many real-world problems rarely follow linear assumptions. This has underscored the need to not simply be judged on performance metrics but to critically analyse results.
One other thing was the clustering which allowed us to see data from a different angle. Understanding four different market segments provided some helpful business insights, including types of listings and pricing strategies. But the low silhouette score indicated that these segments were not well separated. It was a huge learning moment, because it showed that when we get interpretable output from a model, that output might be over-simplifying reality. I learned that model usefulness depends not only on results but also on how accurately those results represent underlying structures.
Broader Implications
The talks on AI writers and machine learning risks deepened my perspective beyond technical implementation. I am now more conscious of concerns about hallucinations, bias and over-relying on automated systems. A crucial insight for me was that machine learning models produce conclusions in response to certain statistical tendencies, as opposed to understanding. This results in a discrepancy between real intelligence and dependability. This distinction can be disastrous in the professional world, particularly in high-risk domains. Now I think of machine learning systems as tools that require continuous human oversight instead of autonomous capabilities.
Collaboration and Contribution
Team project was my key learning project, from collaboration perspective. Working in a virtual setting necessitated the coordination and communication with one another, and also knowing the roles of people. There were some challenges in getting different approaches and the consistency between analysis and reporting in mind. But that was a great learning curve, yet it also sharpened my skill of combining the views and adding value to a common goal, as well. I learned that technical work in machine learning is rarely done in isolation and that collaboration is essential for producing meaningful outcomes.
On a personal note, I felt that I contributed to the paper by adding some clarity of analysis and interpretation of results. Instead of merely presenting the output, I sought to understand its meaning and limitations. And this is consistent with more general aspiration to go from technical deployment to applied comprehension. I do have room for improvement, particularly in exploring more advanced modelling techniques and deepening my statistical intuition.
Professional Development and Action Plan
The module has also helped in my career development, too. It enhanced many important skills, such as critical analysis, problem solving and interpretation of data. I also improved my ability to articulate technical aspects clearly, through written discussions and collaborative work. Most importantly, it highlighted the importance of addressing both ethical and practical dilemmas in the application of machine learning projects.
Moving forward, I would look back on how I have integrated this experience with my understanding of machine learning in the future. I will aim to place greater emphasis on understanding data before modelling, carefully evaluating model assumptions, and considering the broader context in which systems are deployed. I am more conscious of also the need for explainability and transparency, especially when things that impact us in the real world scenarios in sensitive and highly important enviroments.
Conclusion
This module was a balanced learning experience that blended hands-on skill learning with critical reflection and collaborative skills. It was an eye opener to my original assumptions, increased my knowledge of machine learning, gave me skills I can take to practice professionally.
