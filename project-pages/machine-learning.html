Machine Learning
Applying machine learning techniques in real-world contexts, with a focus on data quality, model evaluation, and critical interpretation of results.
Learning Outcomes
Across this module, I developed an integrated understanding of machine learning that combines technical knowledge, critical evaluation, and professional practice.
Throughout the units, I built knowledge of key machine learning techniques, including linear regression and clustering methods such as K-Means and DBSCAN. I learned not only how these algorithms function, but also their assumptions, strengths, and limitations. For example, regression modelling demonstrated how multiple features interact to explain outcomes, while clustering revealed underlying structures in data, albeit with limitations when applied to continuous, real-world datasets. This strengthened my ability to critically appraise model suitability rather than relying solely on performance metrics.
A key theme across all units was the importance of data. I developed a deeper understanding of how dataset quality, feature selection, and distribution directly influence model performance and reliability. Working with real-world data highlighted challenges such as missing values, imbalance, and weak correlations, reinforcing the need for careful preprocessing and critical interpretation of results.
My individual contributions to the team project focused on analytical reasoning and interpretation of findings. I actively engaged in shaping insights from regression and clustering outputs, ensuring that results were contextualised and limitations clearly identified. This contributed to a more balanced and realistic evaluation of the business problem.
As a member of a development team, I strengthened my ability to collaborate in a virtual environment, align with shared objectives, and integrate different perspectives into a coherent outcome. This experience emphasised the importance of communication, role clarity, and structured teamwork in technical projects.
Finally, the module had a strong impact on my professional development. It shifted my perspective from viewing machine learning as purely technical to understanding it as a socio-technical discipline requiring ethical awareness, critical thinking, and responsible application.
